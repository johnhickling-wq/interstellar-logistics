Second edition. The Board reviewed the first printing and found it wanting: thirty line styles are not nine beautiful objects. Herein instead: three corridors, three vessels and three relay beacons, worked up to the standard of the planets — soft, layered, quietly luminous — plus the specimen letterforms (Plate IV) carried over unchanged. Tap a specimen to mark it approved, then press COPY REQUISITION. The adjustments above each plate apply to every specimen on it. Nothing here alters the game.

PLATE I · HYPERSPACE CORRIDORS

Three patterns of channel, shewn at length between two surveyed worlds with vessels passing in both directions — corridors are infrastructure, not traffic, so nothing in the channel flows one way. Each keeps the corridor livery; the swatch control previews any single paint.

PLATE II · VESSELS & POD BARGES

Three vessels of the line — coastal steamers that took a wrong turn into hyperspace. Consignments attend the vessel in open orbit rather than racked on deck, and each towed pod keeps its own small orbit astern. Shewn at roughly three times service scale.

PLATE III · RELAY BEACONS

Three patterns of nebula relay, on station in ionised weather with the corridor passing through — harbour furniture for deep space. The lamp takes the corridor's colour; the instrument keeps company brass.

PLATE IV · SPECIMEN LETTERFORMS

Thirty pairings of display and text letterform, from faces already resident on respectable machines (the company will not be paying font licences). A specimen marked substituted is not installed on THIS machine and is being shewn in its stand-in — judge it on a device that has it.
